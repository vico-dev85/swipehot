# Research: Content Page Conversion Architecture (Organic Traffic → Roulette/Affiliate)

## Context

I'm building a live cam roulette platform with two revenue paths:
1. **Roulette engagement** — users come to the site, enjoy the roulette, and build a habit (retention-first)
2. **Chaturbate affiliate conversions** — users click through to the white label (www.xcam.vip), sign up, and spend tokens

The content engine generates static HTML model pages and blog posts that will capture organic Google traffic. These visitors are **cold traffic** — they searched for something, landed on a content page, and have never seen the roulette before.

**The conversion challenge:**
- Roulette visitors are already engaged (they're swiping, watching, having fun). CTAs work because the user is in an active session.
- Content page visitors are passive readers. They landed from Google, they're scanning, and they might bounce in 10 seconds.
- Different psychology, different conversion strategy needed.

**My current model page structure:**
- Nav bar (site name, links to roulette and models index)
- Live video embed (if model is online) or thumbnail (if offline)
- Model name, stats, tags
- Single CTA button: "Watch [Model] Live" → affiliate link
- 400-500 word bio
- Related models grid
- Footer with legal links

**Revenue model:** 100% Chaturbate affiliate commissions. No ads, no subscriptions, no paywall.
